Introduction to Quaternions

Quaternions are one of those things that people see as black magic. They are viewed as unintuitive, both in using them and in concept. The fact that they are four-dimensional complex numbers does not ease the confusion, either.

This chapter will focus mostly on getting familiar with quaternions, using them in actual programming, and get to know when - and when not to - use them.

If you've heard of quaternions before, and you're into programming, then you have probably heard about them in terms of quaternion rotation. These types of rotations have several advantages over matrix rotation:

  • Avoiding gimbal lock
  • Cheaper to store (4 numbers compared to 16)
  • Cheaper to conatenate
  • Cheaper to fix rounding errors
  • Spherical linear interpolation
  • Although we have to convert from quaternion back to matrix, it is a relatively cheap operation
